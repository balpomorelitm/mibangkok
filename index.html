Arrival: SIM & Transport

  • Buy a SIM card (AIS or TrueMove) at the airport the moment you land. Don't wait until you reach the city.
  • With mobile data you can book a Grab or Bolt right away. You skip the taxi queues, the "broken meter" scams, and the stress of explaining directions.

Getting Around

  • Taxis: Always demand "Meter, please." If they say it's broken or offer a fixed price (usually double), get out immediately. Don't negotiate.
  • Motorbike taxis (GrabBike): Bangkok traffic is infamous. A bike is the only way to cross the city during rush hour. Feels scary at first but it's safe, dirt cheap, and effective. Two people = book two bikes.
  • Buses: No. 3 hours for 5 km in 40Â°C heat. Not worth the savings.
  • River boats: Use the Orange Flag boats (~16 THB). The Blue Flag boats are tourist traps â€” same route, 5Ã— the price.
  • Tuk-tuks: Good for photos, bad for transport. Hot, loud, and overpriced. If you really want to ride one, negotiate hard before getting in.

Food, Drinks & 7-Eleven

  • Ice is safe. It's produced industrially. Locals use it daily, and I never had a problem.
  • Tap water is NOT safe. Always drink bottled.
  • 7-Eleven is on every corner and essential: A/C to recover from the heat, toasted sandwiches (ham & cheese, croissant) heated on the spot â€” surprisingly good, cheap, and a lifesaver late at night â€” plus decent coffee.
  • Utensils: Spoon (right hand) and fork (left hand). The fork only pushes food onto the spoon. Chopsticks are for noodle soups only.

Money & Scams

  • Carry cash. Cards work in malls, but you need cash for street food, local shops, and transport.
  • "The Grand Palace is closed": If a friendly local on the street tells you a temple or the Palace is closed for a ceremony/cleaning/holiday, it's a lie. They want to put you in a tuk-tuk and take you to a suit or gem shop. Ignore and keep walking.

Culture & Law

  • Temples: Strict dress code. Shoulders and knees must be covered. Keep a scarf or sarong in your bag just in case.
  • Head: The most sacred part of the body. Never touch anyone's head, not even children's.
  • Feet: The lowest part. Never point your feet at a person or a Buddha statue. Don't put your feet up on chairs.
  • The Royal Family: No jokes. LÃ¨se-majestÃ© laws are strict. Do not criticize the King or the monarchy, not even online. Don't step on coins or banknotes if they fall â€” they bear the King's image.
  • Vaping: Illegal. Police can fine you heavily or threaten jail to extract a bribe. Leave the vape at home.
  • Cannabis: Currently legal, but smoking in public spaces can get you fined. Use it only in designated areas.

Miscellaneous

  • Mosquitoes: Dengue is real. Don't bring repellent from home â€” it won't work here. Go to a 7-Eleven and buy the pink or orange bottle of Soffell. It smells nice (lemongrass or floral) and actually works in the tropics.
  • Rain: When it pours, forget the umbrella (useless in a tropical storm). Buy a cheap plastic raincoat at 7-Eleven.
